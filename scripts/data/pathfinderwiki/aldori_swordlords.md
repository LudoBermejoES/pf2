The **Aldori swordlords**, sometimes written **Aldori Swordlords**, arose when famed swordsman [Baron Sirian Aldori](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sirian_Aldori "Sirian Aldori") agreed to train a select group of pupils in his dueling techniques. They ruled [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland") for generations, each as prickly and impulsive as the order's founder. They are known for their mastery of the [Aldori dueling sword](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Aldori_dueling_sword "Aldori dueling sword"). Though they are considered some of the finest swordfighters in the [Inner Sea region](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Inner_Sea_region "Inner Sea region"), they are also obsessed with personal standing and honor.

## Members

_See also: [Category:Aldori swordlords/Members](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Category:Aldori_swordlords/Members "Category:Aldori swordlords/Members")_

An Aldori swordlord is a swordfighter practised in the art of dueling who has been taught in the ways of the Aldori school of swordplay established by Baron Aldori. This style is perfect for duels or flashy combat, but can also be used for practical fighting as well. Most of the swordlords are prideful and determined to be the best, leading them to challenge themselves at every opportunity.

### Affiliation

Those who wish to become a swordlord must take the last name of Aldori, swear to the [Aldori swordpact](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Aldori_swordpact "Aldori swordpact"), and demonstrate skill at swordplay by winning a duel using only a [sword](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sword "Sword") in the presence of an Aldori swordlord or experienced exile.

## Recent history

Many veterans of the [Fifth Mendevian Crusade](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Fifth_Mendevian_Crusade "Fifth Mendevian Crusade") have traveled to Brevoy to seek tutelage with the Aldori swordlords since the closing of the [Worldwound](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Worldwound "Worldwound"). They are often mentally scarred and hardened individuals, defined by their long fight against the [demons](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Demon "Demon"), and do little as a group to lessen Brevoy's already volatile national mood.
