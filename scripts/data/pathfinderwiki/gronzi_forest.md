The **Gronzi Forest**, also known as "The Forest" to the locals of [Brevoy](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Brevoy "Brevoy"), is the only woodland of any size within that nation. It covers [House Medvyed's](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Medvyed "House Medvyed") lands and sweeps from the [Icerime Peaks](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Icerime_Peaks "Icerime Peaks") to the shores of [Lake Reykal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_Reykal "Lake Reykal"), the former border between the nations of [Issia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Issia "Issia") and [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland"). Illegal hunting and woodcutting goes on around the forest's outskirts—particularly in its west—even though its resources are ostensibly protected by the Brevic Crown. The parts of the forest near the capital of [New Stetven](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/New_Stetven "New Stetven") have largely been clear-cut to rebuild and expand the city. Its woodcutters must travel ever further to the north and west to find unspoiled stands of [lumber](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lumber "Lumber").

## Inhabitants

As of [4710 AR](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/4710_AR "4710 AR") the forest is menaced by a bandit chieftain known as [Duma the Sly](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Duma_the_Sly "Duma the Sly"), who raids merchants, travelers, and tax-collectors. The chieftain gives part of his proceeds to the poor, so the people love him, even as [King Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Noleski_Surtova "Noleski Surtova") and the army oppose his efforts.

The forest is also home to communities of [gnomes](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gnome "Gnome"). These enclaves are considered uncivilized and shamanistic by typical gnomish standards and mostly keep to themselves. [Taiga giants](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Taiga_giant "Taiga giant") also live in the Gronzi, having settled there after the opening of the [Worldwound](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Worldwound "Worldwound") cut them off from populations farther west.
