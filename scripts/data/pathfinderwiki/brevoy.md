**Brevoy** (pronounced BRE-voy) is a nation in far northeastern [Avistan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Avistan "Avistan") on the verge of tearing itself apart. The political entity known as Brevoy was once two vastly different nations locked in a constant state of war before being conquered by a foreign despot: [Choral the Conqueror](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Choral_Rogarvia "Choral Rogarvia"). After centuries under the rule of Choral and his descendants, they suddenly all disappeared, and Brevoy must now decide its own destiny.

## History

Brevoy is a relatively young nation, having only existed since [4499 AR](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/4499_AR "4499 AR"). The history of Brevoy before this time is the history of two often-warring nations: [Issia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Issia "Issia") and [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland"). The coming of [Choral the Conqueror](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Choral_the_Conqueror "Choral the Conqueror") changed all this. After securing the defeat and surrender of the two nations, Choral christened his family [House Rogarvia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Rogarvia "House Rogarvia"). Choral only ruled his new land for a decade before disappearing, leaving his descendants to rule, and using the threat of the conqueror's return and his fearsome dragon allies to maintain their power. The dragons seemingly disappeared after Brevoy's creation, but they returned once to lay siege to [Skywatch](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Skywatch "Skywatch"), an observatory-cum-fortress held by those still loyal to [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland").

The Rogarvians' rule ended with the mysterious disappearance of every member of House Rogarvia in [4699 AR](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/4699_AR "4699 AR") (an event known as the [Vanishing](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Vanishing "Vanishing")), leaving Brevoy's noble families to pick up the pieces and fall back into their centuries-old disagreements. Eventually [House Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Surtova "House Surtova") gained the upper hand and [Noleski Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Noleski_Surtova "Noleski Surtova") rules fragile Brevoy today.

### Legend of Choral's conquest

A legend concerning the arrival and conquest of Choral is told in Brevoy that enhances the real history. It tells that Choral arrived at [Needle Lake](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Needle_Lake "Needle Lake"), the current [Lake Reykal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_Reykal "Lake Reykal"), with a ragged band of soldiers and knights numbering just over three hundred. After his arrival he declared the creation of a new nation called Brevoy that would encompass much of the land that was then Issia and Rostland. This declaration was almost completely ignored by both nations as neither perceived Choral as a threat.

Rostland eventually had to deal with Choral when he began sending tax collectors from his settlement of [New Stetven](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/New_Stetven "New Stetven") to force the citizens to support his usurping government. They sent a small army to deal with the proud warlord. Rostland's army seriously underestimated Choral's cunning, and their army walked into an ambush. They were lured into a river canyon south of New Stetven where Choral unleashed his secret [red dragon](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Red_dragon "Red dragon") allies. Their flames devastated the Rostlandic army trapped in the canyon with no way to escape and there were few survivors. After this, the dragons rampaged across much of Rostland proper, forcing its leaders to surrender within days. Seeing the devastation Choral caused in Rostland, the Issian lords surrendered immediately, declaring themselves to be a part of his new aristocracy.

### Actual history of conquest

The truth is that Choral arrived in Brevoy via crossing the [Lake of Mists and Veils](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_of_Mists_and_Veils "Lake of Mists and Veils") from [Iobaria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Iobaria "Iobaria"). There he met with [Lord Nikos Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Nikos_Surtova "Nikos Surtova") of Issia, who immediately surrendered in the face of his large army. In exchange, he and the rest of [House Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Surtova "House Surtova") were allowed to retain their prestige and possessions and became vassals to House Rogarvia. Only then did Choral turn his attention to [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland") and the [Aldori swordlords](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Aldori_swordlords "Aldori swordlords"), who did fall in much the same way as the legends recount.

## Geography

[![](/w/images/thumb/2/22/Map_of_Brevoy_and_its_neighbors.jpg/330px-Map_of_Brevoy_and_its_neighbors.jpg.webp)](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/File:Map_of_Brevoy_and_its_neighbors.jpg)

A geographical map of Brevoy and its neighbors.

Brevoy is bordered to the west by wild [Numeria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Numeria "Numeria"), to the south by the unstable [River Kingdoms](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/River_Kingdoms "River Kingdoms"), and to the east by [Iobaria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Iobaria "Iobaria") and the continent of [Casmaron](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Casmaron "Casmaron"). Its landscape differs markedly from north to south, and this change marks the old boundary between the kingdoms of old Issia and Rostland. The north is mostly rugged hills that are unsuitable for agriculture. South of the massive [Gronzi Forest](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gronzi_Forest "Gronzi Forest"), the [Rostland Plains](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland_Plains "Rostland Plains") are rolling fertile grassland that are the breadbasket of the entire nation, and contain the largest tracts of arable land in all of northeastern Avistan.

It can take days to travel between small settlements and longer between larger population clusters. Most of the towns and cities are located at the bases of mountains, along the lakes, or beside the riverways.

Other important geographic features are the [Lake of Mists and Veils](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_of_Mists_and_Veils "Lake of Mists and Veils"), which forms Brevoy's northern border, and the [Icerime Peaks](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Icerime_Peaks "Icerime Peaks") on its eastern border with [Old Iobaria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Old_Iobaria "Old Iobaria"). Other points of interest include:

-   [Golushkin Mountains](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Golushkin_Mountains "Golushkin Mountains") on Brevoy's western border with [Numeria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Numeria "Numeria")
-   [Acuben Isle](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Acuben_Isle "Acuben Isle"), which hosts the city of [Winterbreak](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Winterbreak "Winterbreak") and borders [Winterbreak Bay](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Winterbreak_Bay "Winterbreak Bay")
-   The city of [Eagle's Watch](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Eagle%27s_Watch "Eagle's Watch") on the majestic [Mount Veshka](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Mount_Veshka "Mount Veshka")
-   The melted [Valley of Fire](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Valley_of_Fire "Valley of Fire") where [Aldori](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Aldori_swordlords "Aldori swordlords") rebels had their last stand against [Choral the Conqueror](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Choral_the_Conqueror "Choral the Conqueror")
-   Brevoy's [Lake Reykal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_Reykal "Lake Reykal"), where just over 200 years ago the capital of New Stetven was built on the ruins of the old [Taldan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Taldor "Taldor") settlement of Stetven
-   The highly inaccessible [Claw Point](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Claw_Point "Claw Point") in the far north in Brevoy bordering Iobaria
-   The city of [Restov](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Restov "Restov") in the south on the [Shrike River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Shrike_River "Shrike River")
-   The once-sealed city of [Skywatch](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Skywatch "Skywatch") mysteriously reopened in [4719 AR](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/4719_AR "4719 AR"), but all of its inhabitants seem to have recently disappeared

### Rivers

Major rivers in Brevoy include the [Awzera River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Awzera_River "Awzera River") that flows east into Lake Reykal across the lands of [House Garess](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Garess "House Garess") and [House Lebeda](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Lebeda "House Lebeda"), the [East Sellen River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/East_Sellen_River "East Sellen River") that flows south out of the [Icerime Peaks](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Icerime_Peaks "Icerime Peaks") through the [Gronzi Forest](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gronzi_Forest "Gronzi Forest"), into [Lake Reykal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_Reykal "Lake Reykal") and then down south past New Stetven into the [River Kingdoms](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/River_Kingdoms "River Kingdoms"), and the Shrike River that flows west out of the [Hills of Nomen](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Hills_of_Nomen "Hills of Nomen"), meets Restov, then flows south through the [Stolen Lands](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Stolen_Lands "Stolen Lands"), finally joining with the [East Sellen River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/East_Sellen_River "East Sellen River") in the River Kingdoms.

## Government

[![](/w/images/thumb/4/41/Political_map_of_Brevoy_and_its_neighbors.jpg/330px-Political_map_of_Brevoy_and_its_neighbors.jpg.webp)](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/File:Political_map_of_Brevoy_and_its_neighbors.jpg)

A political map of Brevoy and its neighbors.

Brevoy is ostensibly ruled as a hereditary monarchy from the capital city of New Stetven. House Rogarvia ruled Brevoy for exactly 200 years, until the entire family vanished in the middle of the winter of 4699 AR. No evidence was found to indicate why or how they disappeared. The country is currently ruled by King Noleski Surtova of [House Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Surtova "House Surtova"), who issued a public declaration of his ascent to the throne on 21 [Kuthona](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Kuthona "Kuthona"), 4699 AR, due to his being of the line of Nikos Surtova and Myrna Rogarvia. At his side sits his sister, [Natala Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Natala_Surtova "Natala Surtova"), who reigns as an unofficial Queen, ostensibly only until the bachelor—King Noleski—marries, something which the populace has been hoping he will do. The monarch lives in the Ruby Fortress and sits on the [Dragonscale Throne](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dragonscale_Throne "Dragonscale Throne").

House Surtova has only maintained its rule by allying with former enemies and using fear of the return of House Rogarvia as a tool to unite Brevoy and its [seven noble families](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Noble_Families_of_Brevoy "Noble Families of Brevoy"). Most of Issia supported the ascension, but Rostland has done so reluctantly. Still, some Houses, such as [House Orlovsky](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Orlovsky "House Orlovsky"), only acknowledge Noleski as reigning Lord Regent and diplomatic relations are growing tense.

Increasingly, it appears that Brevoy is on the verge of collapsing back into two separate nations. To try and prevent this, Noleski created the [Dragonscale Loyalists](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dragonscale_Loyalists "Dragonscale Loyalists"), a group of personally selected agents duty-bound to flush out traitors and conspirators who oppose his rule amongst the nation's noble courts.

### Noble families

Many of Brevoy's noble families can trace their lineage back before the invasion of Choral the Conqueror. Others were barbarians, raiders, or pirates who were elevated to nobility for pledging fealty to the Conqueror. Males lead each house, with the eldest son inheriting his father's titles and estates. Other sons may receive something, but there is no law that requires this to be significant. Women can be regents for male heirs until they turn 15 and regents often work behind the scenes to influence the lord. Noble families are relatively large, with most preferring to ensure at least two sons. Since Brevoy's founding, there have been a lot of marriages of alliance among the major houses. Today, the seven major houses are:

-   [House Garess](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Garess "House Garess"), motto: "Strong as the Mountains"
-   [House Lebeda](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Lebeda "House Lebeda"), motto: "Success Through Grace"
-   [House Lodovka](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Lodovka "House Lodovka"), motto: "The Waters, Our Fields"
-   [House Medvyed](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Medvyed "House Medvyed"), motto: "Endurance Overcomes All"
-   [House Orlovsky](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Orlovsky "House Orlovsky"), motto: "High Above"
-   [House Rogarvia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Rogarvia "House Rogarvia"), motto: "With Sword and Flame"
-   [House Surtova](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/House_Surtova "House Surtova"), motto: "Ours Is the Right"

A recent alliance between House Surtova and House Lebeda, which would have symbolically united Rostland (Lebeda) and Issia (Surtova), fell apart when Natala Surtova accused [Lord Lander Lebeda](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lander_Lebeda "Lander Lebeda") of treason. Lander Lebeda subsequently disappeared and [Elanna Lebeda](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Elanna_Lebeda "Elanna Lebeda") left out of solidarity.

### Military

Brevoy's military is organised into several regiments, called [Talon Armies](/w/index.php?title=Talon_Army&action=edit&redlink=1 "Talon Army (page does not exist)"), most of whom are located in Issia. The [Crown Knights](/w/index.php?title=Crown_Knights&action=edit&redlink=1 "Crown Knights (page does not exist)") are the nation's finest cavalry and the [Stetven Shield](/w/index.php?title=Stetven_Shield&action=edit&redlink=1 "Stetven Shield (page does not exist)") serves the capital as its home guard. The mercenary hill folk known as the [Company of the Red Claw](/w/index.php?title=Company_of_the_Red_Claw&action=edit&redlink=1 "Company of the Red Claw (page does not exist)") are also loyal to the throne. King Surtova may call on other noble houses to provide trained soldiers and peasant conscripts as well as the naval power of privateers from [Port Ice](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Port_Ice "Port Ice").

### Foreign relations

Relations between governments (such as they are) in this part of northern Avistan are limited. Brevoy and Mendev maintain diplomatic ties with one another, but Brevoy only has basic, nominal dies with Numeria, [Razmiran](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Razmiran "Razmiran"), or the fractured River Kingdoms. The Brevic government has ranged from being unwelcoming to openly hostile to Razmiran's attempts to spread its faith beyond its borders, as [Razmir's](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Razmir "Razmir") priests are known to foment civil unrest wherever they go.

## Economy

Being so far north, winters are long and harsh. The short spring and summer seasons require the populace to farm and fish just enough to keep food on the table. Brevoy's only extensive arable land is located in Rostov and is one of the region's major exporters of foodstuffs. Issia's sailors control most trade and keep most of the profits for themselves. This has generated considerable resentment among the Rostlanders, who seek to have the wealth distributed more equally. The Issians counter that their ships and trade agreements generate most of the nation's wealth, and argue that those profits need to be "reinvested" in their fleets, lest they are overtaken by foreign competitors.

Brevoy's main imports are cloth, books, finished goods, and [spices](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Spice "Spice"), while common exports include [copper](/w/index.php?title=Copper&action=edit&redlink=1 "Copper (page does not exist)"), fur, [fish](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Fish "Fish") and shellfish, grain, [iron](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Iron "Iron"), [salt](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Salt "Salt"), and [timber](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Timber "Timber"). [Stone](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Stone "Stone") for building is uncommon, so most buildings are constructed from wood; the few notable exceptions include the [Ruby Fortress](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Ruby_Fortress "Ruby Fortress") and the [Bulwark of Gorum](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Bulwark_of_Gorum "Bulwark of Gorum").

The closing of the [Worldwound](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Worldwound "Worldwound") in [4718 AR](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/4718_AR "4718 AR") at the end of the [Fifth Mendevian Crusade](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Fifth_Mendevian_Crusade "Fifth Mendevian Crusade") has increased trade along the [Lake of Mists and Veils](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_of_Mists_and_Veils "Lake of Mists and Veils"), and the need to resettle the formerly [demon-plagued](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Demon "Demon") land has driven up the demand for Brevoy's seed stock and hardy livestock. The [Whispering Tyrant's](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Whispering_Tyrant "Whispering Tyrant") ascent in the [Gravelands](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gravelands "Gravelands") and relocation to the [Isle of Terror](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Isle_of_Terror "Isle of Terror") in [Lake Encarthan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_Encarthan "Lake Encarthan") has also had a positive economic effect on Brevoy, as it has driven much of the trade away from that formerly busy waterway and toward the [Sellen River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sellen_River "Sellen River"), which originates in Brevoy.

## Inhabitants

The peasants of Brevoy are the largest part of the population and owe taxes and fealty to one of the house lords. Their life consists of working from dawn to dusk, farming if they live in [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland"), and either fishing or mining if they live in [Issia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Issia "Issia"). The man of the house may visit the local tavern in the evening. During the winter when fishing or farming cannot be done, household chores fill the days. The peasants try to live simply, support their families, and avoid the notice of nobles and priests.

Taldan [humans](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Human "Human"), many descended from early explorers, make up most of Brevoy's population, but the nation's broad cultural tolerance results in many ethnicities calling Brevoy home. This includes lost [Kellid](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Kellid "Kellid") clans from bordering [Numeria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Numeria "Numeria") and descended from Choral the Conqueror's armies, migrating [Varisians](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Varisian "Varisian") passing through, and [Chelaxian](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Chelaxian_\(human_ethnicity\) "Chelaxian (human ethnicity)"), [Keleshite](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Keleshite "Keleshite"), [Tian](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Tian "Tian"), and [Ulfen](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Ulfen "Ulfen") visitors and outcasts. Brevoy also hosts notable populations of [dwarves](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dwarf "Dwarf") (particularly in [Brunderton](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Brunderton "Brunderton")), [aiuvarins](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Aiuvarin "Aiuvarin"), [dromaars](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dromaar "Dromaar"), and [halflings](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Halfling "Halfling").

The closing of the Worldwound and the end of the [Mendevian Crusades](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Mendevian_Crusades "Mendevian Crusades") has brought idle warriors to Brevoy. Many of them have signed up as mercenaries for the great houses or begun to study with the Aldori swordlords. These soldiers often are traumatized by their experiences fighting demons and can be quite prone to violence, adding to Brevoy's already volatile national mood.

### Languages

Having received waves of migration from all over Avistan, the people of Brevoy speak a large number of languages depending on their own ethnicity. These include [Hallit](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Hallit "Hallit"), [Skald](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Skald "Skald"), [Varisian](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Varisian "Varisian"), and of course the common language of that part of the world, [Taldane](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Taldane "Taldane"). Strangely, a significant minority of people also speak [Draconic](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Draconic "Draconic"), which is quite unusual for [non-dragons](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon") to be conversant in.

## Religion

[Abadar](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Abadar "Abadar") is the most unifying religious force in Brevoy. Many respect him and all have interactions with his followers each time they travel to the markets. Merchants and tradespeople are Abadar's most diligent worshipers in Brevoy, and his temples are places of trade and justice. Those who bear Abadar's golden key are often given recognition as neutral arbiters or judges.

[Gorum's](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gorum "Gorum") household priests often serve Brevoy's nobility and often walk around dressed in gaudy dark red tabards.

[Pharasma](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Pharasma "Pharasma") is most beloved by Brevoy's common people. Her clerics are the least pretentious and serve as midwives, healers, and mortician-monks.

Although Abadar, Gorum, and Pharasma are the most commonly revered [gods](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/God "God"), [Erastil](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Erastil "Erastil") is sometimes worshiped in more rural areas and scattered [Lamashtan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lamashtu "Lamashtu") cults tenaciously endure crusades to exterminate them.
