The city of **New Stetven** is located on the southern banks of [Lake Reykal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lake_Reykal "Lake Reykal") (also known as Needle Lake) in central [Brevoy](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Brevoy "Brevoy") and serves as the nation's capital. The city has been the seat of power in Brevoy since the warlord [Choral the Conqueror](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Choral_the_Conqueror "Choral the Conqueror") founded it over the ruins of the [Taldan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Taldan "Taldan") settlement of [Stetven](/w/index.php?title=Stetven&action=edit&redlink=1 "Stetven (page does not exist)") in [4499 AR](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/4499_AR "4499 AR") after uniting the separate nations of [Issia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Issia "Issia") and [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland") under his rule.

New Stetven is a bustling trade city that acts as a hub for exporting [Brevoy's](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Brevoy "Brevoy") rich supplies of grain, [fish](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Fish "Fish"), [timber](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Timber "Timber"), and ore to the rest of the northern kingdoms, such as [Mendev](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Mendev "Mendev") and [Numeria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Numeria "Numeria"), and down the [East Sellen River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/East_Sellen_River "East Sellen River") to the [River Kingdoms](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/River_Kingdoms "River Kingdoms"). Food from [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland") sustains many of the northern realms, where constant bandit raids or invading [demonic](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Demon "Demon") armies make farming difficult. In exchange for these much-needed goods, imports of exotic items from all corners of [Avistan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Avistan "Avistan") and beyond, such as rare Numerian [skymetals](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Skymetal "Skymetal") and [ancient Osiriani](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Ancient_Osirion "Ancient Osirion") relics, are common in New Stetven's rough-and-tumble marketplaces.

The city, which has made prodigious use of [wood](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Wood "Wood") in constructing everything from mansions, to streets, to forts, is also known as "The City of Wooden Palaces." Due to the prodigious number of wooden structures within its walls, the city boasts several volunteer fire brigades that protect its buildings—citizens join these brigades mostly out of a sense of civic pride. There are but two notable stone structures in New Stetven: the [Ruby Fortress](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Ruby_Fortress "Ruby Fortress"), which houses the [Dragonscale Throne](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dragonscale_Throne "Dragonscale Throne") sits, and the [Bulwark of Gorum](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Bulwark_of_Gorum "Bulwark of Gorum"), a temple to the [Lord of Iron](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gorum "Gorum").

Snow and slush cause the streets to turn to mud for most of the year.
