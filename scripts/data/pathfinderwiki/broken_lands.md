# Broken Lands

> Descargado: 2026-04-16

[![](/w/images/thumb/2/25/Sarkoris_Scar.jpg/250px-Sarkoris_Scar.jpg.webp)](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/File:Sarkoris_Scar.jpg)

The ragged lands of the [Sarkoris Scar](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sarkoris_Scar "Sarkoris Scar").

The **Broken Lands** are the northeastern region of [Avistan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Avistan "Avistan") composed of the nations of [Brevoy](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Brevoy "Brevoy") (itself divided into the distinct areas of [Rostland](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rostland "Rostland") and [Issia](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Issia "Issia")), [Mendev](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Mendev "Mendev"), [Numeria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Numeria "Numeria"), [Razmiran](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Razmiran "Razmiran"), the [River Kingdoms](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/River_Kingdoms "River Kingdoms"), and [Sarkoris](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sarkoris "Sarkoris"). It is a land defined by struggle and scarcity. Its cities lack the prestigious [artistic](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Art "Art"), academic, and cultural institutions found throughout much of the rest of the [Inner Sea region](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Inner_Sea_region "Inner Sea region"). The societies of the Broken Land are often marked by severe inequality that limit social advancement, and its people must often struggle just to survive.

## Geography

Much of the region's scarcity is caused by its lack of arable land, be it the [demon](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Demon "Demon")\-tainted, broken tors of the [Sarkoris Scar](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sarkoris_Scar "Sarkoris Scar"); the cold, [irradiated](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Radiation "Radiation") and windswept steppes of Numeria; or the [mosquito](/w/index.php?title=Mosquito&action=edit&redlink=1 "Mosquito (page does not exist)")\-infested swamps of the River Kingdoms. The little fertile soil there is can be found in southern Brevoy, along parts of the [Sellen River](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Sellen_River "Sellen River"), and in small basins scattered around Razmiran, the River Kingdoms, Mendev, and Numeria. The lack of foodstuffs often supports despotic regimes, such as the one found in Razmiran, where [Razmir](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Razmir "Razmir") rules with near-total dictatorial power.

## Inhabitants

The most commonly found peoples in the Broken Lands are [humans](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Human "Human") of [Kellid](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Kellid "Kellid") or [Taldan](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Taldan "Taldan") extraction, in addition to [dwarves](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dwarves "Dwarves"). The most frequently spoken [languages](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Language "Language") are [Chthonian](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Chthonian "Chthonian"), [Draconic](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Draconic "Draconic"), [Hallit](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Hallit "Hallit"), [Skald](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Skald "Skald"), [Taldane](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Taldane "Taldane"), and [Varisian](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Varisian_\(language\) "Varisian (language)"). Popular [deities](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/God "God") worshipped include [Abadar](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Abadar "Abadar"), [Cayden Cailean](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Cayden_Cailean "Cayden Cailean"), [Desna](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Desna "Desna"), [Gorum](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Gorum "Gorum"), [Iomedae](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Iomedae "Iomedae"), and [Lamashtu](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Lamashtu "Lamashtu").

## Economy

Brevoy stands as a prominent exporter of food within the region. Rostland contributes [timber](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Timber "Timber"), grain, fruit, vegetables, and livestock, whereas Issia specializes in dried and salted [fish](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Fish "Fish"), pickled [clams](/w/index.php?title=Clam&action=edit&redlink=1 "Clam (page does not exist)"), and freshwater [pearls](/w/index.php?title=Pearl&action=edit&redlink=1 "Pearl (page does not exist)"). Notably, the peoples of Brevoy import finished goods, cloth, [spices](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Spice "Spice"), and [books](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/List_of_written_works "List of written works"). In contrast, Mendev largely relies on imports for its survival necessities. Numeria exports [skymetal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Skymetal "Skymetal"), [illicit technology fragments](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Technology "Technology"), [hides](/w/index.php?title=Hide&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hide (page does not exist)"), teeth of the enigmatic wasteland creatures, and live, captive specimens, while primarily importing food, [alcohol](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Alcoholic_beverages "Alcoholic beverages"), and [jewelry](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"). Razmiran, another key food exporter, also deals in cloth, lumber, and fine woodwork, importing [alchemical](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Alchemy "Alchemy"), [herbal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Herbal "Herbal"), and [medicinal](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Medicine "Medicine") supplies.

## Politics

The political history of the Broken Lands is marked by countless calamities and various strongmen who took advantage of it. These calamities include the opening of the [Worldwound](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Worldwound "Worldwound"), the crashing of an immense [starship](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Destiny "Destiny") during the [Rain of Stars](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Rain_of_Stars "Rain of Stars"), and the invasion of [dragon](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon")\-enhanced armies from [Iobaria](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Iobaria "Iobaria") in [Brevoy](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Brevoy "Brevoy"). Each of these events caused untold decades, centuries, or millennia of suffering.
